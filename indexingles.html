Area Attraction

Biodiversity of the Osa Peninsula

The Osa Peninsula is home to 2.5% of the world's biodiversity and more than 50% of the biodiversity in Costa Rica, it is the most extensive continuous forest of the Pacific Coast in Central America, it also represents one of the last tropical rain forests with sufficient extension to be able to support viable populations of Central American flora and fauna.

Its climate and physiography are important factors that determine the great biological diversity and a diverse scenario in terms of landscapes, ecosystems, and species. It has endemic species, possibly as a consequence of the geographic isolation of the Osa Peninsula, including twenty species of trees.

General Information of the Osa Peninsula

Located on the southwest coast of the Pacific Ocean, it is located south of the province of Puntarenas. Despite being a relatively small area, it contains a large number of tropical habitats: rainforests, coastal wetlands, mountain forests, etc., it also includes the Térraba-Sierpe wetlands, several micro-basins in the Costeña Line, the basins of the Sierpe, Esquinas and Coto Colorado rivers, and Burica point. They are considered one of the most beautiful areas of Costa Rica

General Information of Corcovado National Park

Guarded by the state, Corcovado National Park is one of the emblematic parks of the region, recognized worldwide for its scenic beauty and because a large number of flora and fauna species are observed there.

It is home to approximately 400 species of birds, 140 species of mammals, 115 species of amphibians, 500 species of trees, and 6,000 species of insects. The animals that stand out the most are the jaguar, puma, crocodile, tapir, dendrobatidae (frogs), the harpy eagle and sea turtles. Other protected areas are; the Terraba-Sierpe National Wetland, the Piedras Blancas National Park, the Golfo Dulce Forest Reserve, and the others in the region.

General Information of the Golfo Dulce

The Dulce Gulf is a small tropical Gulf of Costa Rica located on the southern coast of the country, in the waters of the Pacific Ocean. Administratively it is divided between the cantons of Osa and Golfito.

The inlet begins on the Pacific Ocean side of Costa Rica, and runs slightly north before turning west. In the westernmost part is the city of Rincón de OSa. The bay separates the Osa Peninsula from the mainland of Costa Rica

A good part of the perimeter of the gulf is made up of protected forested areas, such as the Golfo Dulce Forest Reserve to the west, the Piedras Blancas national park to the northwest and the Corcovado national park, located to the south; These areas cover an area of ​​more than 56,000 hectares of protected tropical forest, as well as an important marine area to protect the marlin and hammerhead sharks, among other species.
